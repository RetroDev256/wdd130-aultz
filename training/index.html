Excercise

Getting in shape for Buhurt requires a combination of strength, endurance, agility, and combat-specific conditioning. The training should focus on building muscle strength, cardiovascular endurance, flexibility, and combat skills, all while also preparing you for the intense physicality of full-contact combat. (Here's a breakdown of a 3-month, 6-month, and yearlong routine to prepare you for Buhurt.)

Progressive Training

Structured 3-month, 6-month, and 12-month programs to build strength, endurance, and combat skills.

Combat-Specific Workouts

Focus on weapon drills, sparring, and functional fitness tailored for Buhurt combat.

Recovery & Nutrition

Proper diet, hydration, and recovery strategies to enhance performance and prevent injuries.

Excercise Routines

3 Month Routine

  • The goal of this phase is to build a solid foundation in strength, cardiovascular fitness, and overall endurance while also starting to introduce combat-specific exercises.

    • Weekly Workouts


  • Monday: Full-body strength training (focus on compound movements)
  • Tuesday: Cardiovascular conditioning + light sparring
  • Wednesday: Mobility & flexibility + core workout
  • Thursday: Full-body strength training (focus on explosiveness)
  • Friday: Endurance training (HIIT, running, or cycling) + combat drills
  • Saturday: Rest or active recovery (light stretching, yoga, or walking)

    • Key Workouts


  • 1. Strength Training (full Body)

  • Squats (3 sets of 8-10 reps)
  • Deadlifts (3 sets of 6-8 reps)
  • Push-ups or Bench Press (3 sets of 10-12 reps)
  • Pull-ups or Lat Pulldowns (3 sets of 8-10 reps)
  • Overhead Press (3 sets of 8 reps)
  • Farmerâ€™s Walk (3 rounds of 30-40 meters)

  • 2. Cardiovascular Conditioning

  • Running: 30 minutes of steady-state running or interval sprints (30 seconds fast, 1 minute slow)
  • Cycling: 45 minutes at a moderate pace

  • 3. Combat Drills

  • Basic swordplay, shield maneuvers, and footwork
  • Shadow sparring: movement, blocking, and striking without an opponent
  • Breathing and maintaining focus during combat situations

6 Month Routine

  • The goal of this phase is to increase the intensity of workouts and focus on combat-specific strength and conditioning while maintaining general fitness.

    • Weekly Workouts


  • Monday: Strength training (full body with a focus on functional movements)
  • Tuesday: High-intensity interval training (HIIT) + combat drills
  • Wednesday: Flexibility and mobility + core strengthening
  • Thursday: Strength and explosive power (e.g., Olympic lifts)
  • Friday: Endurance (long runs, rowing, swimming) + sparring or full-body combat practice
  • Saturday: Active recovery (yoga, swimming, light cycling)
  • Sunday: Skill practice (Buhurt drills, weapons practice, and tactical combat)

    • Key Workouts


  • 1. Strength Training (Functional & Compound)

  • Front Squats (3 sets of 6-8 reps)
  • Deadlifts (3 sets of 4-6 reps)
  • Clean and Press (3 sets of 5 reps)
  • Dumbbell Rows (3 sets of 8-10 reps)
  • Kettlebell Swings (3 sets of 15 reps)
  • Turkish Get-ups (3 sets of 5 per side)

  • 2. HIIT Training

  • Battle Ropes (30 seconds on, 30 seconds off for 10 minutes)
  • Tire Flips or Sled Push (30 seconds on, 30 seconds off for 10 minutes)
  • Burpees (3 sets of 15-20)

  • 3. Endurance

  • Running: 5-10 km steady-state or interval training
  • Swimming: 45 minutes of interval training in the pool
  • Rowing: 30-40 minutes with high intensity

  • 4. Combat Drills

  • Partner Sparring (light contact, focusing on technique and footwork)
  • Heavy Bag Training (simulate strikes with swords or axes)
  • Shield Wall Drills (blocking and movement in groups)

12 Month Routine

  • The goal of this phase is to achieve peak performance, improve specific combat skills, and enhance endurance under stress.

    • Weekly Workouts


  • Monday: Strength training (focus on power and hypertrophy)
  • Tuesday: Combat-specific training (sparring, weapons drills, footwork)
  • Wednesday: Cardiovascular endurance (long sessions) + flexibility
  • Thursday: Strength + explosive training (Olympic lifts, kettlebell)
  • Friday: Sparring (full-contact drills, Buhurt scenarios) + agility training
  • Saturday: Rest or active recovery (yoga, swimming)
  • Sunday: Combat drills (focus on decision-making and fatigue resistance)

    • Key Workouts


  • 1. Strength Training (Power & Hypertrophy)

  • Squats (4 sets of 6-8 reps)
  • Deadlifts (4 sets of 4-6 reps)
  • Overhead Press (4 sets of 6-8 reps)
  • Pull-ups (4 sets of 10-12 reps)
  • Farmers Walks (4 rounds of 40 meters)
  • Kettlebell Swings (4 sets of 20)

  • 2. Combat-Specific Training

  • Sparring: Full contact, realistic fight scenarios
  • Weapon Drills: Swords, axes, shields; focus on fluidity, timing, and reaction
  • Tactical Training: Movement patterns simulating battle scenarios

  • 3. Agility and Reaction Time

  • Cone Drills (5-10 minutes of short sprints, direction changes)
  • Box Jumps (3 sets of 10)
  • Lateral Skater Jumps (3 sets of 15)

  • 4. Endurance (Mental Toughness)

  • Long Runs: 10-15 km at a moderate pace, or 60 minutes of swimming or cycling
  • Obstacle Course: Military-style obstacle courses for toughness and agility
