Body Armor

  1. Body Armor

    This includes a breastplate and backplate made of metal, such as steel, to protect the torso. It may also involve additional elements like a cuirass, or a chainmail hauberk, which covers the body and arms for full protection.

  2. Arm & Leg Protection

    1. Pauldrons

      Large shoulder guards that protect the upper arms.

    2. Vambraces

      Arm armor that covers the forearms.

    3. Greaves

      Metal shin guards that protect the lower legs.

    4. Sabatons

      Armored footwear or boots designed to protect the feet.

  3. Gloves

    Protective gloves, often made of leather with metal reinforcement, to safeguard the hands and fingers.

  4. Shield

    A large, sturdy shield (usually a kite or heater shape) for defense, often made of wood and metal or fully metal, depending on the regulations of the event.

Underlayers

  1. Gambeson

    Beneath the metal armor, fighters are required to wear padded garments to absorb impacts and reduce the risk of injury. This includes a padded gambeson or jacket, which provides additional comfort and protection.

  2. Neck Protection

    A coif or collar, made of padded fabric or metal, is used to protect the neck from strikes.

Weapons

  1. Sword

    A longsword or one-handed sword with a blunt edge, typically made of steel but designed to avoid injury during combat.

  2. Axe

    A battle axe, designed with a blunt edge, is also a common weapon in Buhurt.

  3. Mace

    A weapon with a spiked or flanged head, also used in Buhurt competitions.

  4. Polearms

    Weapons such as a halberd or spear may be used, depending on the rules of the specific competition.
